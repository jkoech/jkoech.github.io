Introduction: Who We Are

NKN transporters is a limited liability partnership that was incorporated in 2015. We provide local and international road haulage services throughout East Africa. Our teams have years of experience and are ready to meet the requirements of our customers.

NKN's primary business is in transportation of fuel and petroleum products. Additionally, NKN has diversified into transportation of dry good; both edible and non-edible.

NKN aims to maintain high quality of service thanks to the state of the art, reliable technology, wellmaintained vehicles and skilled staff. Currently NKN has 4 full-time employees and 15 long and short haul drivers.

NKN has a fleet that includes SCANIA, Mercedes, Fh, DAF and ISUZU vehicles that meet the required environmental, and health and road safety requirements as stipulated by the Government of Kenya, and the East African Customs and Border Control.

Currently NKN has a fleet of 18 trucks of which 9 are dedicated to transportation of petroleum and 9 on transportation of bulk material.

The average age of our vehicles are 3 years, which ensures high reliability of services provided t our customers. Online monitoring of all vehicles is a pride and a matter of fact at NKN transporters.

Governance | Leadership & Company Values

As a young, stable and growing company, we purpose to create portfolios of diverse logistic services that define, accommodate and complement the future economic growth of East Africa. NKN transporters recognize that effective and sustainable transportation service is necessary in supporting and catalysing economic growth especially in all physical and tradable goods. We therefore aim to ease and accommodate our customers by ensuring that the burden of transportation is taken care of by us.

As a well-experienced firm, we dwell on ensuring that customer services provided to our customers meet the needs of the customer. This is in turnaround of service delivery, assuring safety of the products during transportation, and that products delivered are in the good conditions they were found in.
